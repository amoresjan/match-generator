# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Two audiences share the same session URL:

**Hosts (admins)**: the person who organized the group — typically one person per session. They create the session, add players, generate rounds, and occasionally override a match. They're on their phone courtside, managing chaos between games. They want it to just work with no learning curve.

**Players (guests)**: everyone else in the group. They open the session link on their own phone to see who they're playing, when they're sitting out, and how rounds are going. They glance at it for 2 seconds between points, then put their phone away.

## Product Purpose

Rally removes the mental math of fair rotation in casual group sports. It tracks who has played with and against whom, generates balanced 1v1 or 2v2 matchups, and ensures nobody sits out too long. Success means every player feels the rotation is fair, the host spends zero energy on logistics, and the group just plays.

## Brand Personality

Playful, casual, celebratory.

Rally is the friend who shows up with a whiteboard and takes care of all the bookkeeping so everyone else can just have fun. It has personality (hot streaks, win animations, sport emoji) but never gets in the way of the game.

## Anti-references

- **Fantasy sports and gambling apps**: dark-mode neon, aggressive stats-first layouts, high-pressure visual density.
- **Enterprise SaaS dashboards**: cold navy palettes, corporate typefaces, data tables for their own sake.
- **Fitness tracking apps**: metric overload, progress rings, relentless motivational copy ("Crush your goals!").
- **Social media feeds**: infinite scroll, likes/comments, algorithmically-ordered content, notification anxiety.

## Design Principles

1. **Get out of the way.** The UI exists to get people on the court. Every screen should answer one question immediately: what do I need to know right now?

2. **Glanceable over granular.** Players check their phone for 2 seconds between points. Visual hierarchy must surface the most important thing at a glance. Details are secondary.

3. **Celebrate what matters.** Hot streaks, first wins, being called to court — these moments should feel good. Delight is part of the product, not decoration.

4. **Personal but shared.** The same session feels different for each player (their court, their partner highlighted) while remaining a shared experience everyone can see.

5. **Delight without noise.** Animations and personality add energy without overwhelming a casual social setting. Nothing should blink, demand attention, or feel urgent when the moment isn't.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Sport-specific color themes must maintain sufficient contrast on both light and dark backgrounds. Reduced-motion users should see instant state changes without animation energy loss. Color is never the sole indicator of state (icons and labels always accompany color).
